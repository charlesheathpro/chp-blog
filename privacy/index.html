Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 8, 2026

Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Colorado Home Protection ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you visit blog.coloradohomeprotection.net (the "Blog"). We take your privacy seriously and are committed to being transparent about our practices.

Information We Collect

The Blog is a read-only resource. We do not require you to create an account or submit personal information to access any content.

  • Server logs: Our hosting provider (Netlify) automatically records standard server log data, including IP addresses, browser type, referring URLs, and pages visited. This data is used solely for operational and security purposes and is governed by Netlify's Privacy Policy.
  • Contact by email: If you email us directly, we receive your email address and the contents of your message. We use this only to respond to your inquiry.

Third-Party Services

The Blog uses the following third-party services, each with their own privacy practices:

  • Netlify — Website hosting and serverless functions. Netlify Privacy Policy →
  • Google Blogger — Content management system. Blog posts are authored and stored on Google's Blogger platform. Google Privacy Policy →
  • Google Fonts — Typography. Google Fonts may log requests made by your browser. Google Privacy Policy →

Cookies

The Blog does not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics cookies. Netlify may set technically necessary cookies for site operation and performance. We do not sell, share, or use your browsing data for advertising purposes.

Links to Other Websites

The Blog contains links to our main website (coloradohomeprotection.net) and occasionally to external sources. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of any third-party websites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any site you visit.

Children's Privacy

The Blog is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.

Data Security

We take reasonable precautions to protect information transmitted to or from the Blog. All traffic is encrypted via HTTPS. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Continued use of the Blog after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
